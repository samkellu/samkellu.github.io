History and Explanation

Hard Disks or hard drives became the dominant form of secondary storage in computers after their invention in the late 1950's by IBM. They are prized for their reliable function, high capacity and low cost in manufacturing. Throughout the decades, science and manufacturing advancements have allowed for capacity to increase from 3.75MB in 1956 on the IBM 350 unit (which is the size of a car) to 12TB hard disks today such as the Seagate Enterprise line of drives (which are 3.5 inches wide).

How Do They Work?

Hard Disk drives use an array of platters coated in a thin layer of magnetically conductive material to store data. It does this by using a set of arms to change the direction of magnetization on the spinning platter. The arms then read the transitions in magnetization and encode them using a scheme to convert the transitions into binary bits of data.
